How does the iOS Accelerometer Work?

The iOS accelerometer measures the acceleration applied to the device. It contains a micro-electromechanical system (MEMS) that senses acceleration along the x, y, and z axes. This data is used to detect the orientation and movement of the device.

The accelerometer works based on the principles of Newtonian physics, using changes in capacitance to detect movement. This allows iOS devices to perform tasks such as screen rotation, motion-based gaming, and step counting.
